Workflows

Overview

Almagest provides ten research workflows. Each one launches an agentic loop that searches NASA ADS, fetches paper metadata and abstracts, and synthesizes findings into a structured Markdown report saved to outputs/.

CommandDescription
deepresearchMulti-agent investigation — the flagship workflow
litSystematic literature review with consensus mapping
sourceAll literature for a named object or sky position
reviewSimulated peer review with severity-graded issues
auditPaper-to-code reproducibility audit
replicateStep-by-step replication plan (+ optional execution)
compareSide-by-side comparison with agreement/conflict matrix
draftFull paper-style draft with inline citations
autoresearchAutonomous hypothesis → search → refine loop
watchRecurring monitor for new papers on a topic
coauthorFriendly co-author review delivered as a personal email
Workflow

deepresearch

deepresearch is the flagship Almagest workflow. It issues multiple ADS searches from different angles, extracts findings from the most relevant papers, and synthesizes everything into a structured research brief with inline citations and a BibTeX bibliography.

Usage

shell
almagest deepresearch "dark matter halo formation at high redshift"
almagest deepresearch "mechanisms of AGN feedback quenching star formation"
almagest deepresearch "JWST constraints on galaxy formation at z > 10"

# Steer the investigation with extra context
almagest deepresearch "AGN feedback quenching" \
  --context "Focus on low-mass galaxies (M* < 10^10 Msun) and observational evidence only"

How it works

  • 01
    Search

    Multiple ADS queries are issued — by keyword, citation rank, date, and review articles — to ensure broad coverage from different angles.

  • 02
    Extraction

    Abstracts and metadata are fetched for the top papers. Key claims are traced back to specific bibcodes.

  • 03
    Synthesis

    Findings are cross-referenced and organized into a structured brief: background, key findings, open questions.

  • 04
    Citation

    Every claim is labeled with its source bibcode. Unverifiable claims are marked [UNVERIFIED] or [INFERRED].

Output

  • SummaryConcise overview of the topic and state of the field.
  • BackgroundHistorical context and foundational papers.
  • Key FindingsMain results with inline citations.
  • Open QuestionsActive debates and gaps in the evidence.
  • BibliographyBibTeX block for all cited papers.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-deep-research.md

Customization

Narrow topics produce focused, evidence-dense briefs. Broad topics produce survey-style overviews. Use --context to restrict scope, specify a redshift range, prioritize certain instruments, or seed the search with bibcodes you already know.

lit

lit produces a systematic literature review from primary sources: foundational papers, key milestones, the current consensus, active debates, and open gaps. Ideal as background research before starting a new project or writing a paper introduction.

Usage

shell
almagest lit "stellar feedback in dwarf galaxies"
almagest lit "fast radio bursts progenitor models"
almagest lit "integral field spectroscopy IFU surveys"

# Include specific papers you want covered
almagest lit "oxygen abundance gradients" \
  --context "Include IFU surveys only. Make sure to cover:
  - 2014A&A...563A..49S (CALIFA survey)
  - 2018MNRAS.481.2299S (MaNGA gradients)"

How it works

The agent issues multiple ADS queries — most-cited papers for seminal work, doctype:review for review articles, and recent date-ranked results for new developments. It fetches full metadata and abstracts for the top papers, then synthesizes a structured review mapping the consensus and flagging active debates.

Output

  • IntroductionMotivation and definition of the topic.
  • Foundational WorkSeminal papers that established the field.
  • Current ConsensusWhat is well-established and broadly agreed upon.
  • Active DebatesConflicting results and competing interpretations.
  • Open QuestionsWhat remains unknown or underexplored.
  • BibliographyBibTeX for all cited papers.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-lit-review.md

Customization

Restrict to a time window ("Focus on work published after 2015"), a specific instrument ("CALIFA and MaNGA only"), or a redshift range. Seed it with bibcodes you want included and ask the agent to explain how they fit into the overall narrative.

source

source searches NASA ADS for all literature about a specific astronomical object or sky position. It resolves object names via SIMBAD/NED and synthesizes a complete overview with a discovery timeline, instrument coverage, and consensus findings.

Usage

By object name
almagest source "NGC 1068"
almagest source "M87" --topic "jet kinematics"
almagest source "Crab Nebula" --topic "pulsar wind nebula"
By sky position
almagest source --ra 40.669 --dec -0.014 --radius 5arcmin
almagest source --ra 187.706 --dec 12.391 --radius 2arcmin --topic "radio jet"
Radius formats — 5arcmin, 30arcsec, 0.5deg, or a plain number (= arcmin). Maximum radius is 1 degree.

How it works

The agent runs a broad ads_search_object to find all papers about the object via SIMBAD/NED resolution. If a topic is given, a second filtered call narrows results. For positional queries, a cone search is performed. The top 8–10 papers are fetched in full and synthesized into a structured report.

Output

  • Object OverviewType, distance, redshift, and notable characteristics.
  • Key PapersTable of top papers with bibcode, authors, year, and citation count.
  • Discovery TimelineChronological key findings with instrument and bibcode.
  • Instrument CoverageWhich telescopes and surveys have observed the source.
  • Consensus & Open QuestionsWhat is established vs. still debated.
  • BibliographyBibTeX for all cited papers.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-source-lit.md

review

review simulates a peer review of a paper. It accepts an ADS bibcode, a local PDF, or both, and produces a structured review with severity-graded issues and an actionable revision plan.

Usage

shell
# By ADS bibcode
almagest review 2023ApJ...950...72C

# Local PDF (preprint, paywalled paper, or your own manuscript)
almagest review --pdf ~/Downloads/my_paper.pdf

# Both — full PDF text + ADS metadata and related-work search
almagest review 2023ApJ...950...72C --pdf paper.pdf

# Guide the reviewer toward specific concerns
almagest review --pdf paper.pdf \
  --context "Consider these related papers:
  - 2021ApJ...910...72C  (metallicity gradients, same method)
  - 2019MNRAS.484.5230R  (IFU methodology reference)
  Check whether systematic errors in abundance calibration are propagated."

How it works

The agent reads the full paper text (from PDF or ADS abstract) and evaluates it against peer review criteria: novelty, methodology, statistical rigor, discussion of limitations, and citation completeness. It then searches ADS for relevant related work to check for missing citations. Each issue is tagged with a severity level.

Output

  • SummaryOne-paragraph assessment of contribution and quality.
  • StrengthsWhat the paper does well.
  • IssuesGraded list: CRITICAL · MAJOR · MINOR · COSMETIC.
  • Missing CitationsRelevant ADS papers that were not cited.
  • Revision PlanNumbered action list of recommended changes.
  • RecommendationAccept / Minor revision / Major revision / Reject.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-peer-review.md

audit

audit checks whether a code repository linked to a paper (GitHub, Zenodo, ASCL) actually reproduces the methods, parameters, and results described in the text. It flags mismatches between the paper and the code.

Usage

shell
almagest audit 2022ApJ...935..167V
almagest audit 2019JOSS....4.1414F

# Focus the audit on a specific claim
almagest audit 2022ApJ...935..167V \
  --context "Check whether the stellar mass function parameters in Table 2
  match the values hardcoded in the fitting routine."

Output

  • Paper SummaryKey methods, parameters, and results as described.
  • Code FoundRepository URL and description.
  • MatchesClaims correctly reproduced in the code.
  • MismatchesDiscrepancies between paper and code, with file locations.
  • MissingSteps described in the paper with no corresponding code.
  • VerdictOverall reproducibility assessment.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-audit.md

replicate

replicate generates a step-by-step plan to independently reproduce the key results of a paper: data access, software environment, expected intermediate outputs, and validation checks. Add --execute to run the first steps in a sandboxed shell.

Usage

shell
# Plan only (safe — no code runs)
almagest replicate 2020MNRAS.499..230B

# Plan + execute
almagest replicate 2020MNRAS.499..230B --execute

# Constrain to your environment
almagest replicate 2020MNRAS.499..230B \
  --context "I only have access to public SDSS data. Target Python 3.11."

Output

  • Data AccessWhere to download datasets and any access restrictions.
  • EnvironmentRequired packages, versions, and setup commands.
  • StepsNumbered plan from raw data to final figures.
  • Expected OutputsWhat each step should produce, with validation criteria.
  • CaveatsPotential obstacles: missing data, stochastic components, hardware requirements.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-replication.md

compare

compare places competing theories, methods, or results side by side and produces an agreement and conflict matrix. Useful for understanding where the literature converges and where it disagrees.

Usage

shell
almagest compare "NFW vs Einasto dark matter density profiles"
almagest compare "ram pressure stripping vs AGN feedback as quenching mechanism"

# Guide the comparison dimensions
almagest compare "NFW vs Einasto dark matter profiles" \
  --context "Focus on: (1) fit quality in dwarf galaxies,
  (2) behaviour at small radii, (3) N-body results post-2015."

Output

  • OverviewBrief description of each approach.
  • Agreement MatrixDimensions on which the sources agree, with citations.
  • Conflict MatrixDimensions on which they disagree, with evidence on each side.
  • VerdictCurrent state: resolved, ongoing, or context-dependent.
  • BibliographyBibTeX for all cited papers.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-comparison.md

draft

draft researches a topic from NASA ADS and writes a full paper-style draft — abstract through conclusions — with inline citations and a BibTeX bibliography. Not submission-ready, but a solid starting point that accelerates writing.

Usage

shell
almagest draft "the role of environment in galaxy quenching at z ~ 0.5"
almagest draft "a review of chemical abundance gradients in nearby galaxies"

# Seed with your own data and target journal
almagest draft "chemical abundance gradients in nearby galaxies" \
  --context "Assume we have MaNGA IFU data for 500 galaxies.
  Emphasise comparison with CALIFA results. Target: ApJ."

Output

  • Abstract200-word structured abstract.
  • IntroductionMotivation, context, and statement of the problem.
  • Background / MethodsReview of prior work and proposed methodology.
  • Results & DiscussionKey findings with inline citations.
  • ConclusionsSummary and future directions.
  • BibliographyComplete BibTeX block.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-draft.md

autoresearch

autoresearch starts from a seed idea and runs an autonomous loop: form a hypothesis, search ADS for evidence, evaluate support, refine, and repeat. It continues until the evidence converges or --max-rounds is reached.

Usage

shell
# Default 5 rounds
almagest autoresearch "galaxy quenching driven by AGN feedback"

# More thorough
almagest autoresearch "origin of the stellar mass-metallicity relation" --max-rounds 10

# Constrain the hypothesis space
almagest autoresearch "galaxy quenching at cosmic noon" \
  --context "Restrict to observational evidence at 1.5 < z < 2.5.
  Prioritise JWST and ALMA results published after 2022."

How it works

Each round the agent states its current best hypothesis, searches ADS for confirming and refuting evidence, updates its confidence score, and refines the hypothesis for the next round. The final report records each hypothesis, the evidence considered, and the confidence trajectory across all rounds.

Note: This workflow performs multiple ADS search rounds. Use --max-rounds 3 for quick explorations to limit API usage.

Output

  • Hypothesis logThe hypothesis at each round with the evidence considered.
  • Evidence summaryPapers supporting and contradicting the final hypothesis.
  • Confidence trajectoryHow support changed across rounds.
  • Final assessmentBest-supported hypothesis and remaining uncertainties.
  • BibliographyBibTeX for all papers consulted.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-autoresearch.md

Workflow

watch

watch registers a saved ADS search and reports new papers each time you run watch run. With --digest, it generates an AI summary of what is new and notable since the last check.

Usage

shell
# Add a watch (topic text becomes the ADS query)
almagest watch add "galaxy quenching"

# Add a watch with a custom ADS query
almagest watch add "IFU metallicity gradients" \
  --query "abs:metallicity abs:gradient (bibstem:ApJ OR bibstem:MNRAS) year:2024-2025"

# Watch a specific author's new papers
almagest watch add "Pillepich new papers" \
  --query "author:Pillepich,A year:2024-2025"

almagest watch list                  # list all registered watches
almagest watch run                   # check for new papers
almagest watch run --digest          # check + generate AI digest
almagest watch remove galaxy-quenching

Output

Without --digest: a plain list of new papers since the last run (bibcode, authors, title, citation count).

With --digest: an AI-written summary grouping new papers by sub-topic, with brief annotations explaining each paper's contribution.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-digest-<date>.md

coauthor

coauthor invites the agent to be a co-author of your draft. It reads the paper as a trusted collaborator and writes a warm, personal email with honest feedback — not a formal referee report. The tone is collegial, the output is a Markdown email.

Usage

shell
# Read a local PDF draft
almagest coauthor --pdf ~/Documents/my_draft.pdf

# Review a published paper by bibcode
almagest coauthor 2023ApJ...950...72C

# Both — full PDF text + ADS metadata
almagest coauthor 2023ApJ...950...72C --pdf draft.pdf

# Focus the feedback on a specific section
almagest coauthor --pdf draft.pdf \
  --context "Pay particular attention to Section 3 and the discussion of systematics."

How it works

The agent reads the full paper text (from PDF or ADS abstract), searches NASA ADS for related work that should be cited or discussed, and checks key claims against the literature. It then writes a personal email as a colleague who genuinely wants the paper to succeed — covering overall impression, what works well, specific concerns explained kindly, and any missing references worth adding.

Difference from review — review produces a structured referee report with severity labels (CRITICAL, MAJOR, …). coauthor writes a friendly email in prose, as a collaborator rather than an anonymous reviewer.

Output

  • To / From / SubjectEmail header with author names (if found) and paper title.
  • Overall impressionA warm opening that acknowledges the work and states the main take.
  • What works wellGenuine strengths of the paper, explained in context.
  • ConcernsHonest feedback on weak points, framed as questions or suggestions rather than verdicts.
  • References to considerPapers found in ADS that might strengthen the discussion.
  • Encouraging closeA positive sign-off that acknowledges the contribution.

Saved to: outputs/<slug>-coauthor-review.md
